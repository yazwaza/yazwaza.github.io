I am a second year Data Science and Economics student minoring in Mathematics at Northeastern University. I was born in Japan and currently live in Singapore. This website will showcase my current portfolio of projects related to my interests in Quantitative Analysis. My academic journey has driven me to explore the intersection of data, markets, and mathematics, fields that I believe are increasingly intertwined in today's financial landscape. I have completed projects spanning a range of topics, including analyzing US Treasuries, applying Graph Theory to Forex markets, and investigating whether major economic indicators serve as reliable predictive tools. Currently, I am working on a project related to predictive markets, exploring how data driven models can anticipate market movements. Through these projects, I have developed strong skills in data analysis, statistical modeling, and financial research. Outside of academics, you can find me on the slopes carving through fresh powder, on the golf course finishing up at double bogey, or in the club playing my set at the DJ booth. These pursuits reflect the same curiosity and drive I bring to my academic work, whether I am reading a market trend or reading a crowd.
