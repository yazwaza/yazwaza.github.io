Below is a collection of quantitative finance projects I have completed as part of my academic and self directed research. Each project reflects my interest in applying data driven methods to real financial markets. My work spans fixed income modeling, foreign exchange analysis, macroeconomic forecasting, and statistical arbitrage. Through these projects I have developed proficiency in Python, statistical modeling, and financial data analysis. I am particularly drawn to areas where mathematics and markets intersect, and where rigorous methodology can uncover insights that intuition alone cannot. Each project has also taught me the importance of understanding the limitations of models, not just their outputs. Markets are complex adaptive systems, and a model that works in one regime can fail completely in another. That lesson runs through all of my work and continues to shape how I approach new problems.

  • US Treasury Yield Curve Modeling — Built a Nelson-Siegel-Svensson model using Nelder-Mead optimization to fit US Treasury yields across 13 maturities. Achieved an R² of 0.99 by processing 2 years of daily data with warm-start initialization and vectorized operations in Python. The model captures short-term volatility as well as long-term curve features like the 10-year dip and 20-year hump, though it struggles with sudden regime changes such as Fed policy shifts and inverted yield curves.
  • Forex Technical Indicator Testing and Dollar Strength Index — Tested whether RSI and CCI indicators could predict USD-CHF price reversals. Found that extreme overbought and oversold readings did not predict reversals; prices continued trending in the same direction. Also built a custom Dollar Strength Index using the Federal Reserve's NEER data across 9 currency pairs, weighted by trade volume. My index achieved an 83% correlation with the Fed's official measure, validating the methodology.
  • NFP Forecasting Using Initial Jobless Claims — Built a model to predict the monthly Non-Farm Payrolls report using weekly unemployment claims. The model showed weak predictive power before COVID with an R² of just 6% and average errors of around 76,000 jobs. During COVID the model completely broke down with errors exceeding 2,700,000 jobs, demonstrating how structural regime changes can render statistical relationships useless.
  • Forex Graph Theory and Triangular Arbitrage — Modeled currency markets as a graph with currencies as nodes and pairs as edges. Identified theoretical triangular arbitrage opportunities and tested them through paper trading with promising initial results. However, real-world implementation revealed significant challenges including bid-ask spreads of 0.3 to 0.5% on exotic pairs, execution latency, and liquidity constraints. The project deepened my understanding of market microstructure and transaction cost modeling.
  • Pairs Trading: JNK and Bitcoin (Current) — Exploring a pairs trading strategy between JNK, a high yield bond ETF, and Bitcoin. The pair was chosen due to my interest in credit markets and the evolving narrative around Bitcoin, including its political dimensions and emerging links to institutional finance. Used cointegration testing to confirm the two assets move together, yielding a cointegration value of 0.91. Followed with regression analysis to establish R² and applied an Ornstein-Uhlenbeck process to calculate the half life of mean reversion.
